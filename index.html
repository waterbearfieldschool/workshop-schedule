Workshop Descriptions

Intro to Mesh Radio

Beginner-Friendly

Learn how to build and deploy your own mesh radio node using LoRa technology. Mesh networks let communities communicate even when cell towers and internet are down - perfect for emergency preparedness, outdoor adventures, or just staying connected with neighbors.

What You'll Learn

  • How mesh radio networks work and why they matter
  • Setting up and configuring a Meshtastic node
  • Connecting with other nodes in your area
  • Best practices for range and reliability

Take Home

Optional: Purchase a ready-to-use radio node kit for $30 to take home and join the local mesh network.

Register for This Workshop

Intro to DIY Off-Grid Power

Beginner-Friendly

Discover how to generate and store your own electricity using solar panels and human power. We'll cover small-scale systems perfect for charging devices, running lights, and building resilience into your home or community space.

What You'll Learn

  • Solar panel basics: sizing, orientation, and wiring
  • Battery selection and safe charging practices
  • Building a portable solar charging station
  • Bike-powered generators (some sessions)

Take Home

Knowledge to build your own system. Component lists and local sourcing tips provided.

Register for This Workshop

Intro to Off-Grid Telemetry

Beginner-Friendly

Build sensors to monitor temperature, humidity, soil moisture, and more - all without relying on cloud services or monthly fees. Perfect for greenhouses, root cellars, beehives, chicken coops, or anywhere you need to keep an eye on conditions.

What You'll Learn

  • Sensor basics: what to measure and why
  • Setting up low-power monitoring devices
  • Viewing your data locally or over mesh radio
  • Practical applications for farms, gardens, and homes

Take Home

Optional: Purchase a sensor kit for $30 to monitor your own space.

Register for This Workshop

Wildcard Workshops

Beginner-Friendly

These rotating sessions cover a variety of practical skills for self-sufficiency and community resilience. Check the calendar for the specific topic each week.

Rotating Topics Include

  • Cold Frames & Hoop Houses - Extend your growing season with simple structures
  • Rainwater Capture - Harvest and store water for gardens and emergencies
  • Temperature Monitoring - Build sensors for greenhouses and freezers
  • Solar Charging Stations - Create phone/device charging kiosks
  • Garden Carts & Fixtures - Build useful outdoor infrastructure

Many workshops offer optional hardware kits (radio nodes, sensors, etc.) for $30. You'll learn with provided equipment during the workshop - purchasing is totally optional.

Virtual Sessions

Some workshops are offered virtually (marked with a Virtual badge on the calendar). For virtual sessions:
